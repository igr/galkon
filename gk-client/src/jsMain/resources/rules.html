The Universe

  • The playing field is a grid with randomly placed planets.
  • Planet A is Player 1's home planet, Planet B is Player 2's and so on.
  • All other planets start as neutral.
  • After placement, players vote to accept or regenerate the layout.

Planet Properties

  • Production — ships generated per turn for the planet's owner.
  • Kill Ratio — combat effectiveness. Higher is better.

Turn Structure

  1. Each player enters fleet orders (hidden from the opponent).
  2. A move: send N ships from Planet X to Planet Y.
  3. Multiple orders per turn are allowed.
  4. Ships are deducted immediately from the source planet.
  5. Sending ships to your own planets is allowed (reinforcements).
  6. After both players submit, the turn resolves.

Ship Movement

  • Ships travel 2 light-years per turn.
  • Ships in transit cannot be recalled.

Production

  • Happens at the end of each turn, after movement and combat.
  • Each owned planet produces ships equal to its production value.
  • Neutral planets do not produce ships.
  • A newly captured planet produces on the same turn it was captured.

Combat

  • If a fleet arrives at a friendly planet, ships are added as reinforcements.
  • Otherwise, a battle occurs.
  • The defender gets a home court bonus.
  • Combat proceeds one round at a time until one side is eliminated.
  • A higher kill ratio means better survival odds.
  • If the attacker wins, they take ownership and adopt the planet's kill ratio.
  • Fleets do not merge before combat — each resolves separately.

Events

  • Random events may trigger after each fleet arrival.
  • Change Production — planet production changes.
  • Change Kill Ratio — planet kill ratio changes.
  • Revolt — flavor text only, no actual effect.

Fog of War

  • You can see enemy planets and their labels.
  • You can see enemy ship counts and stats.
  • You cannot see enemy fleets in transit.

Game End

  • The game ends after the configured number of turns (40).
  • All remaining in-transit fleets are resolved over additional movement cycles.
  • Production continues during fleet resolution.
  • Final scores are then calculated.
